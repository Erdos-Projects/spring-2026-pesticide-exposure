County map — asthma & COPD risk

Colors show relative risk levels for the selected outcome using county predictions from a model trained on pesticide use (plus demographics, cropland, and health covariates), aligned to 2019 CDC PLACES-style outcomes. Toggle Asthma or COPD to recolor; popups show predicted vs. observed where available.

How to interpret “risk percentile”

Each county gets a risk rank and risk percentile based on its predicted prevalence for the selected outcome (Asthma / COPD), compared to all other counties in the map dataset for that year.

  • Higher risk percentile = higher predicted prevalence (e.g., 95th percentile means the county’s prediction is higher than ~95% of counties).
  • Risk rank #1 = highest predicted prevalence (highest relative risk on this map).
  • This is a relative comparison across counties, not an absolute probability of exposure or a clinical diagnosis.

Concerned about pesticides affecting your community?

Pesticide exposure is not distributed equally. Some communities face greater environmental burdens due to where people live, work, and go to school. Use these resources to learn about exposure, explore health data, understand your rights and protections, and find ways to support safer, more transparent environmental policy in your community.
